I became the publisher of "It's a Bunny" when I had to fill out the form to get an ISSN number. There was a field called Publisher that needed a name, so I filled in the blank. Until then, I was just the technocrat amongst the literati responsible for design and informatics. But now I'm a publisher. Then again, on the Web, everyone is a publisher. But it's not every site that gets named a Canuck Site of the Day.

In my ongoing search for the ultimate magazine interface, our little oasis in Cyberia now boasts a virtual table of contents along the left side of major sections, improved graphics, and a stylized 'b' in the upper left hand corner that instantly sends you back to the front page. A fitting environment for some of the remarkable works in this issue. It's finally spring, enjoy.



Finally, our Spring issue is ready for release.

For the time being we are a quarterly publication but we have lofty ambitions. We plan to come out monthly beginning in October. We will also do our best to respond to submissions within two weeks, if at all possible. The launch (I am told) was a success. Yes, I was there, but waited for gossip to circulate just to be sure.

We are in need of short fiction, sudden or postcard fiction, and essays not too self-consciously literary. Please comment. We love the feedback. And thanks for all the support.

East Tennessee, 1968

Our thoughts are like broken furniture,
Hurled in anger against the quiet,
Stirring past some queer yard.
Morning and the paganism of black coffee,
The sky all bursting with light,
The old man sheds its skin. Last night
Quietly knocking in our bones
The queer tremors of seven o'clock,
And there has risen in the oven a human face,
Dark plunges half-way across the room
A hand pries loose a jelly jar, spreads
Thick the goodness on rye toast.

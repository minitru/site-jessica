SHADOW

                    The shadow of our jet
                      flies to the side,
                     child's-hand in size,
                  an incidental image hardly
              noted. But as we begin to descend
            it sidles nearer, enlarging, and as we
            dip toward the runway it grows to become
              the very shape of the plane, under
         our belly a darkness to land on a once-tiny
                   echo we roar down to meet.
